Renewable energy is increasingly seen as the only viable long-term solution to the global energy crisis. Critics of this position argue that the intermittency of solar and wind power makes them unreliable for meeting base-load energy demand. However, this concern, while legitimate, overlooks the rapid advances in grid-scale battery storage technology. Bloomberg NEF (2023) reports that battery costs have fallen by 89% since 2013, making storage solutions increasingly cost-effective at scale. It therefore follows that intermittency, rather than being an insurmountable barrier, represents a technical challenge that is actively being resolved.

Self-Editing Checklist — Argument Quality
  • Does every paragraph have a clear, specific, debatable claim (topic sentence)?
  • Is every claim supported by evidence from a credible, cited source?
  • Have I explained the warrant — the logical connection between evidence and claim?
  • Have I acknowledged and refuted at least one counterargument?
  • Is my argument Logos-dominant, with appropriate hedging?
  • Have I avoided logical fallacies (post hoc, ad hominem, straw man, false dichotomy)?
